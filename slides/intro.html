What is Quality?

  1. A product or service free of deficiencies.

  2. The characteristics of a product or service
    that bear on its ability to satisfy
    stated or implied needs.

  3. Fitness for use.

Terminology

Defect, Fault, Error, Bug
When the execution of a software observed output does not match the expected output
Failure
Result of execution of a fault
Test, Test Case
A set of inputs given to the program and its expected outputs. Inputs may also include preconditions. Outputs may include post-conditions.
Reliability
The probability of failure free operation for a specified time under specific conditions

Terminology

Verification
Ensure that the software implements a specific function correctly.
Are we building the software right?
Validation
Ensure that the software fulfills actual needs of the customer.
Are we building the right software?

What is Software Testing?


“The process of executing a program with the intent of finding faults”
G.J. Myers, “The Art of Software Testing”, 2004.

Can Testing demonstrate
the absence of defects?

NO

... but testing certainly helps to find defects, and most importantly:
it helps to find them early!!!

Why do we test software?

  1. Demonstrate that software behaves as per specifications.
  2. Establish confidence about the correctness of a program.
  3. Find defects in earlier stages. The cost of fixing defects increases with time.
  4. Ensure alignment with customer needs.

Who should do the testing?

Developers are intimately involved with the development of the software and thus is very difficult for them point out errors from their own creations.
B. Beizer, “Software Testing Techniques”. 1990

According to Google:
Developers should write the tests, Testers should provide tools and automation shoud take care of executing.
James A. Whittaker, "How Google Tests Software". 2012

Tests can't guarantee quality by themselves

Software testing is an important, but not the only,
part of quality assurance.

Quality is a reflection of an organization's
